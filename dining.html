  • Travel Time: 👟 20-25 min walk / 🚲 7-9 min cycle.

Dinner (Pasta, Pizza & Red Wine)

  1. La Plume (⭐ 4.5/5)
    • Travel: 👟 25 mins / 🚲 8 mins.
    • Vibe: Gorgeous leafy terrace with lake views.
    • Food: Excellent Carbonara & Grilled Meats.
    • 🍴 Order: The Carbonara, or the tagliata di manzo (sliced rare steak with rocket). Pair with a glass of Lugana or Bardolino.
    • Link: Google Maps
  2. Ristorante Pizzeria da Frank (⭐ 4.4/5)
    • Travel: 👟 25 mins / 🚲 9 mins.
    • Vibe: Unpretentious, lively, family-favourite.
    • Food: Arguably the best thin-crust pizza in town.
    • 🍴 Order: Any thin-crust pizza from the wood oven — the Diavola (spicy salami) for adults, plain Margherita for the kids. Big eaters: the calzone.
    • Link: Google Maps
  3. Raffilù (⭐ 4.5/5)
    • Travel: 👟 22 mins / 🚲 7 mins.
    • Vibe: Elegant terrace right on the canal water's edge.
    • Food: Seafood specialties & great Amarone wine list.
    • 🍴 Order: Spaghetti alle vongole (clams) or the catch of the day; finish with an Amarone by the glass. The plainer pasta al pomodoro keeps the kids happy here too.
    • Link: Google Maps
  4. Trattoria Bella Italia (⭐ 4.5/5)
    • Travel: 👟 10 mins / 🚲 3 mins. (Very Close).
    • Vibe: Rustic and traditional. authentic Veronese feel.
    • Food: Hearty meat ragu, Bigoli pasta.
    • 🍴 Order: The big local specialities — bigoli al ragù d'anatra (thick pasta, duck ragù) and tortellini di Valeggio in butter & sage. The closest proper restaurant to the resort.
    • Link: Google Maps
  5. L'Osteria (⭐ 4.3/5)
    • Travel: 👟 22 mins / 🚲 7 mins.
    • Vibe: Located inside the historic fortress walls.
    • Food: Known for "Pizza bigger than the plate".
    • 🍴 Order: One of the famous giant pizzas (a Margherita or Prosciutto easily feeds an adult plus a child). Great fun, great value — the kids love the size.
    • Link: Google Maps
  6. Bella Vista Ristorante (⭐ 4.2/5)
    • Travel: 👟 20 mins / 🚲 6 mins.
    • Vibe: Large lakefront terrace, very spacious for families.
    • Food: Reliable stone-baked pizza & GF options.
    • 🍴 Order: A stone-baked pizza with a lake view; ask for the gluten-free base if needed. Roomy terrace = easiest with a buggy or three tired kids.
    • Link: Google Maps
  7. Vecchio Mulino Beach (⭐ 4.4/5)
    • Travel: 👟 15 mins / 🚲 5 mins. (Scenic Lake Walk).
    • Vibe: Cool "Beach Bar" restaurant style. Relaxed luxury.
    • Food: Gourmet burgers, pasta, cocktails.
    • 🍴 Order: A gourmet burger and chips (a sure-fire kid win) and an Aperol Spritz at sunset. Come for golden hour by the water.
    • Link: Google Maps
  8. La Taverna di Pulcinella (⭐ 4.1/5)
    • Travel: 👟 25 mins / 🚲 8 mins.
    • Vibe: Neapolitan style (think thick fluffy pizza crusts).
    • Food: Authentic Naples pizza & seafood pasta.
    • 🍴 Order: A proper Neapolitan Margherita (puffy, charred crust) or the spaghetti allo scoglio (mixed seafood) for the adults.
    • Link: Google Maps
  9. Al Canal (⭐ 4.3/5)
    • Travel: 👟 22 mins / 🚲 7 mins.
    • Vibe: Romantic setting on the canal, flower-lined tables.
    • Food: Homemade desserts & wood-oven pizza.
    • 🍴 Order: A wood-oven pizza followed by the homemade tiramisù — leave room, the desserts are the draw here.
    • Link: Google Maps
  10. Il Cantinone (⭐ 4.2/5) - The "Carbonara" Spot
    • Travel: 👟 22 mins / 🚲 7 mins.
    • Vibe: Historic center, lively street atmosphere.
    • Food: Authentic Roman Cuisine. The best Carbonara in town.
    • 🍴 Order: The Roman trio — Carbonara, cacio e pepe (cheese & pepper) and amatriciana. The carbonara is the one to beat.
    • Link: Google Maps

👧 Picky Eaters: What the Kids Will Actually Eat

Italian food is a gift for fussy eaters — almost everything is mild, and "plain" is normal, not rude. Rachel (9) and the twins (7) will find something everywhere. Useful menu words are also on the Phrases page.

  • Pizza Margherita — just tomato & mozzarella. The safest meal in Italy. Ask for "una pizza margherita, per favore". No hidden herbs or spice.
  • Pasta al pomodoro — pasta in plain tomato sauce. The default kids' pasta.
  • Pasta in bianco / al burro — pasta with just butter & parmesan, no sauce. Totally normal to ask for; say "pasta in bianco". Great for the really picky days.
  • Pasta al ragù — the local "bolognese". Most child-friendly meaty pasta.
  • Cotoletta / scaloppine — a thin breaded chicken or veal cutlet (basically a posh chicken nugget/schnitzel). Usually comes with fries.
  • Patatine fritte — chips/fries, available almost everywhere.
  • Pollo / bistecca — plain grilled chicken or steak, no sauce, if they want protein only.
  • Prosciutto e melone — ham with melon; or just "pane" (bread) and grissini (breadsticks) to keep them going while you order.
  • Gelato — the universal peace treaty. One scoop every day; see the Food & Drink page for the best gelaterie.

Handy tips for ordering with kids:

  • Ask for the "menù bambini" (kids' menu) or a "mezza porzione" (half portion) — both are common and cheaper.
  • Pizzerias are the most reliable family bet: fast, cheap, and everyone finds something.
  • Italian food is rarely spicy — only dishes labelled "piccante" or "Diavola" have a kick, so avoid those for the kids.
  • Free tap water isn't standard; bottled "acqua naturale" (still) or "frizzante" (sparkling) is ordered by the bottle.
  • A Crodino (orange, alcohol-free aperitivo) lets the kids join "spritz o'clock" — details on the Food & Drink page.
