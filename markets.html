Almost every town around the lake has a lively weekly open-air market — a fun, free morning out and a highlight if you love a good rummage. Expect stalls of clothes, leather bags and belts, shoes, linen, toys, local cheese and salami, olives, fruit and seasonal produce. Each town has its own day, so you can plan a market into most mornings.

Weekly Market Days Near the Resort

Markets usually run morning to early afternoon (~08:00–13:00). Days are traditional but can change — verify locally (reception or the town’s tourist office) before you set off.

Don’t-Miss Special Markets

  • Peschiera Monday market — the easy one: stroll or cycle in, browse the stalls, then gelato. The perfect gentle morning between bigger days.
  • Antiques & vintage markets — several lake towns hold a monthly antiques/collectors’ market (often a weekend). Ask reception what’s on during your stay.
  • Christmas-in-summer? No — but local sagre (food festivals) pop up through summer with food stalls and music; worth checking the local what’s-on.

Market Tips (with Kids)

  • Go early — best choice, cooler, and easier with little ones before it’s packed.
  • Bring cash in small notes — many stalls don’t take cards (see Essentials).
  • Gentle haggling is OK on clothes/leather if you buy a few items — ask “Mi fa un prezzo?” (can you do me a price?). See the Phrases page.
  • Watch bags and pockets in the crush, and agree a meeting point with the kids.
  • Taste before you buy — cheese and salami stalls usually offer a sample.
  • Pack a tote — great for picnic bits for that day’s lunch.
