Del Garda Village & Camping — Via Marzan 92, 37019 Peschiera del Garda VR. Google Maps
GPS: 45.447816, 10.699870  ·  Our home: Mobile Home "Clivia Medium (4+1)"

A medium-sized, modern, pedestrian-friendly lakeside village on the south-east shore — squarely aimed at young families. It sits right on the Lake Garda promenade, a ~5-minute walk from the water and roughly a 20-minute stroll (or 8-min cycle) into Peschiera old town.

At a glance:

  • Vibe: Quiet, safe, car-light layout — easy for the girls (9, 7, 7) to roam between the pool, mini-disco and home.
  • WiFi: Free throughout the village.
  • Air conditioning: 12 hours/day included in our mobile home; extra usage €1.00/hour (see Booking).
  • Lake: Direct promenade access; the resort is on the flat Peschiera cycle network (see Logistics).

3.1 Swimming Pools

The pool areas are the heart of the village and the kids' favourite — two separate pool complexes, one on each side of the park, so you're never far from water.

  • Layout: Each complex has a large pool for adults/older swimmers plus a shallow children's lagoon with slides and water-play features — 4 pools in total.
  • Kids' lagoon: Max depth ~0.6m with a toddler slide, small water slides and a splash/play area — ideal for 7-year-olds.
  • Water slides: Several small-to-medium slides across the two complexes (great for the girls; not big "white-knuckle" flumes).
  • Relax: Whirlpools, sun decks and loungers around the pools.
  • Pool bar: Serves drinks, cocktails, snacks, fresh fruit and ice cream right by the water.
  • Heating: Pools are not heated — guests report they feel cool on entry but fine once you're in. (Verify on arrival.)

3.2 Bars & Restaurants (On-Site)

You can eat well without ever leaving the gates — handy on the tired arrival night (27 June) and after long pool days.

  • The Garden Risto & Pizza — large, casual two-room pizzeria with counter service. Pizzas, pasta and family staples; guests praise the lasagne and ribs.
  • Plaza Restaurant — the slightly smarter sit-down option (tablecloths, softer lighting) for a nicer family dinner.
  • Pool bar — snacks, fresh fruit, ice cream, coffee and cocktails poolside.
  • Value: Reviewers rate the food well-priced for a resort, with friendly, efficient staff. (Confirm 2026 menu prices on arrival.)

3.3 Shop & Mini-Market

  • On-site Mini-Market: Stocks daily essentials — fresh bread, freshly baked croissants, milk, fruit, drinks, wine and basics for self-catering in the mobile home.
  • Good for: Breakfast supplies and top-ups; prices are convenience-led, so do a bigger shop off-site (see §3.7) for the main stock-up.

3.4 Sports & Activities

Plenty to burn off energy between pool sessions:

  • Tennis and table tennis (ping pong)
  • Beach volleyball
  • Five-a-side football and basketball
  • Gym / fitness area
  • Games room / arcade for rainy spells or post-storm evenings
  • Daytime fitness: The animation team runs sessions such as yoga, spinning and group football. (Exact 2026 schedule posted at reception — check on arrival.)

3.5 Entertainment & Kids Club

  • Animation team: Works through the day with a varied programme of games, sports and activities for all ages.
  • Kids club: Organised children's activities — a great way for Rachel, Jessica and Rebecca to make friends.
  • Mini-disco: The classic evening highlight — singing and dancing for the little ones before bed.
  • Evening shows: Family-friendly entertainment near the bar/plaza.

3.6 Lake Access & Beaches

  • From the resort: The promenade runs along the shore with steps down to the water at intervals — there's no sandy beach at the site itself.
  • Pebble beach (~6-min walk right): Small pebble beach with pedaloes to rent.
  • Lido ai Pioppi Google Maps (~8-min walk left): Pebble beach with a beach bar and pedaloes.
  • Bring aqua/water shoes — the lake shore is stony (this is on the packing list in Activities).

3.7 Nearest Shops & Restaurants Outside the Resort

The resort has two side gates into the neighbourhood and the lakefront. Gate keys/tags need a refundable deposit at reception — worth it for the short walks below.

Supermarkets (for the big shop)

  • Lidl Google Maps — Via Frassino 1. ~10-15 min walk (or short cycle/taxi). Open Mon–Sat 08:00–20:30, Sun 08:30–19:30. (Verify hours June 2026.)
  • Eurospin Google Maps — right beside Lidl on Via Frassino; handy budget alternative.
  • Tip: A scenic lakefront route gets you there; some guests use a campsite/local shuttle. Big range vs. the on-site mini-market.

Restaurants & gelato just outside (Via Bell'Italia / lakefront)

A short walk towards the neighbouring Bella Italia area and the promenade — all family-friendly and close by.

  • Trattoria Bella Italia Carne Google Maps — Via Bell'Italia 4. Traditional Veronese pasta and grilled-meat dishes; rustic room and terrace.
  • Pizzeria La Rocca Google Maps — Via Bell'Italia 29. Wood-fired pizza and Mediterranean dishes by the lake — easy crowd-pleaser for the kids.
  • Bella Italia Pesce (BIP) Google Maps — stylish trattoria for fresh fish/seafood, for a grown-up evening.
  • Gelateria Alle Vecchie Mura Google Maps — Via Bell'Italia 16. Well-loved ice-cream stop on the walk into town.
  • Peschiera old town Google Maps — ~20-min walk / ~8-min cycle: the bigger choice of restaurants, bars and gelato inside the historic walls (full picks in Dining).

3.8 Guest Tips & Hints

Gathered from recent family reviews — worth knowing before you go.

  • Grab loungers early: In peak weeks, get to the pool before ~10:00 for a sunbed. There's a policy against "reserving" loungers with towels for long periods (≈30 min), so don't rely on bagging one early and leaving.
  • Two pool complexes = options: If one side is busy, the other is usually quieter — the complex furthest from reception tends to be calmer.
  • Pools run cool: They're unheated; fine once you're in, but factor it in for the youngest.
  • Pack water shoes: The lake shore and nearby beaches are pebbly, not sandy.
  • Get the gate tags: Pay the deposit for gate keys/tags — they unlock the short walks to the lake (~10 min) and into town (~20 min).
  • Eat in on arrival night: After the evening flight + transfer (27 June), the on-site pizzeria/pool bar is the easy first-night option.
  • Self-cater smart: Use the mini-market for breakfast/bread, and do the main shop at Lidl/Eurospin.
  • Expect afternoon storms: Short, intense thunderstorms are common (see Activities) — the games room/indoor bar is the bolt-hole.
