Planning 3–4 runs of about 5 km over the trip? You’re in a great spot: flat lakeside promenades and a traffic-free river path run right from Del Garda Village Map. Here are four routes from the gate, plus how to fit them around the holiday.

Beat the Heat — Read First

  • Go early. It’s often 25°C+ by 9am. Aim for 06:30–08:00 — cooler, quiet paths, lovely light on the lake.
  • Hydrate before and after; carry a handheld bottle for anything over ~4 km in the heat. There are public fountains in Peschiera’s old town to refill.
  • Sun & safety: cap, sunglasses, light-coloured kit, sun cream even early. Carry your phone and a bit of ID.
  • Share the path: keep right, expect cyclists and pedestrians, and take care on damp cobbles after a storm.

Four Routes from the Resort Gate

Route A — Pacengo Lakefront Out-&-Back (~5 km)

  • Route: turn left (north) out of the gate onto the lake promenade and run to the little harbour at Pacengo Map, then turn back.
  • Surface: flat, paved, hugging the water. Shade: partial.
  • Best for: an easy, scenic first run. Go before 8am — it fills with walkers later.

Route B — Mincio River / Ciclovia (~5 km)

  • Route: head into Peschiera to Porta Brescia and join the Ciclovia Peschiera–Mantova Map; run south along the River Mincio ~2.5 km to the first weir, then loop back.
  • Surface: gravel/paved, tree-lined. Shade: good. Traffic: none — safest route.
  • Best for: the hottest morning — shade and a flat, soft surface.

Route C — Fortress Walls Loop (~5–6 km)

  • Route: run to town and loop the historic UNESCO Venetian fortress walls Map, then home.
  • Surface: mixed paved/cobbles. Shade: partial. Great views over the moat and canals.
  • Best for: a sightseeing run — ease off on the cobbles.

Route D — Southern Lakefront (~5 km)

  • Route: from the gate, follow the promenade south towards the Lido/Sirmione side of the bay Map and back.
  • Surface: flat, paved. Shade: limited — best very early.
  • Best for: open lake views and a fast, flat 5 km.

Suggested Plan for the 10 Nights

Slot runs onto rest/pool days, not big theme-park days when you’ll already be on your feet.

What to Pack

  • Trainers with a bit of grip (Route B is gravel), 3–4 sets of light running kit, a cap and sunglasses.
  • A handheld water bottle, anti-chafe balm, and a small amount of cash/card for a post-run espresso or water.
