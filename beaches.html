Lake Garda’s beaches are mostly pebble or grass, not sand — lovely, clean and swimmable, but pack water shoes for everyone (it’s on the packing list). Here are the best family spots within easy reach of Del Garda Village, plus how lake swimming works with children.

Right by the Resort

  • Resort promenade: the village sits on the shore with steps down to the water — handy for a quick dip, but there’s no sandy beach at the site itself (see Resort).
  • Lido ai Pioppi Map — pebble beach with a beach bar and pedaloes, ~8-min walk.
  • Pebble beach (~6-min walk): small, with pedaloes to rent — an easy first paddle.

Free Beach or Paid Lido?

  • Free (spiaggia libera): most lake beaches are free to walk onto — bring your own towels/shade.
  • Paid lido: some beach clubs charge for sun-loungers, parasols and a cleaner, serviced area with a bar and (sometimes) a pool — nice for a planned beach day.

Lake Swimming with Kids

  • Water shoes for everyone — the pebbles are hard on bare feet.
  • It shelves quickly in places and there are usually no lifeguards — keep the twins in arm-bands and stay close.
  • Morning is calmest; the afternoon breeze (and the odd storm) can push up little waves — fine for paddling, but watch the youngest.
  • Bring: a UV pop-up/sun shade, plenty of water, and a dry bag for phones (all on the packing list).
  • Prefer the pool? The resort has two pool complexes with a shallow kids’ lagoon — often the easiest win on a busy beach day (see Resort).

Beach facilities and any lido charges vary season to season — confirm locally.
