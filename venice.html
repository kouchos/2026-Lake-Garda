A plan for two families spending a full day together in Venice. We travel in from Lake Garda (by train from Peschiera); the other family comes from Union Lido Map on the Cavallino coast (by ferry across the lagoon). The two routes land at opposite ends of Venice, so the trick is picking one clear meeting point and a relaxed, kid-friendly loop between them. Kids on the day are roughly 7–12, so this is paced for short legs, plenty of gelato stops and one booked sit-down lunch.

Both routes are step-free-ish but boat-heavy — keep buggies to a minimum (Venice is all bridges and steps). Fares and times below are flagged for verification nearer the date.

Our Route (Lake Garda → Venice)

  • Catch an early regional train from Peschiera del Garda Map (~2.5 km from Del Garda Village) on the Milan–Venice line. A ~08:00 departure puts you into Venezia Santa Lucia Map around 10:00.
  • For speed, take a regional to Verona Porta Nuova then a Frecciarossa/Italo on to Venice (~1h15 that leg) — book ahead for the best fare.
  • Return regional fare is roughly €24–30 pp; children 4–11 travel reduced (often free with an adult on regional trains), under 4 free. Validate paper regional tickets before boarding.

Their Route (Union Lido → Venice)

  • From the Union Lido area it’s a short hop (ATVO/ACTV bus or drive) to Punta Sabbioni Map, the lagoon ferry terminal at the tip of the Cavallino peninsula.
  • Take ACTV ferry line 14 (or 15) straight across to San Zaccaria Map, a 2-minute walk from St Mark’s Square — about 35 min on the water with a gorgeous run past the lagoon islands.
  • A day vaporetto pass (~€25 pp, kids reduced) covers this boat and any Grand Canal hops during the day — worth it for both families.

Where to Meet

Best meeting point: the bottom of the Campanile (bell tower) in Piazza San Marco Map. It’s impossible to miss, the Union Lido family arrive 2 minutes away at San Zaccaria, and we reach it easily by Vaporetto Line 1 or 2 down the Grand Canal from the station (a 30–40 min ride that is itself a highlight). Aim to meet at 11:00 — forgiving enough for both journeys.

Quieter back-up: if San Marco is a crush, switch the rendezvous to Campo Santo Stefano Map, a big open square with cafés and room for kids — 10 min walk from San Marco, on our way in from the station. Agree the fallback before the day, and swap phone numbers / share live locations, as mobile signal can wobble between the tall buildings.

This is deliberately unhurried — two big sights, one long lunch, lots of pottering. With 7–12-year-olds, less really is more in the heat.

Lunch — Book This in Advance

For a group of two families (roughly 4 adults + 5 kids) you want somewhere that takes bookings, has space and a kid-friendly menu, and isn’t wedged onto a tourist-trap square. Top pick first:

  • ★ Birraria La Corte Map — pizza, pasta and grills on the big open Campo San Polo, with outdoor tables and room for kids to breathe. Takes group bookings online — the easy, reliable choice for this day. ~10 min walk from Rialto.
  • Trattoria alla Madonna Map — a classic Venetian seafood institution near Rialto; big, bustling, good value. Phone ahead to hold a table for a group (busy and cash-friendly).
  • Rossopomodoro Venezia Map — dependable Neapolitan pizza chain near San Marco; bookable, fast, fuss-free and a safe bet with fussy younger eaters.

Booking tip: reserve a week or two out for ~12:45, note the head-count including children and ask for highchairs if needed. Confirm the day before. Prices and booking policies flagged for verification.

Kid-Pleasers Along the Way

  • The boat rides are the main event — the ferry crossing and the Grand Canal vaporetto beat any museum for a 7–12-year-old.
  • Campanile lift for the bird’s-eye view; feeding-free pigeon spotting in the square.
  • Rialto market fish and produce stalls (mornings are liveliest).
  • Mask & glass shops — pick a small Murano-glass trinket as a souvenir.
  • Gelato count: aim for at least two. It is the law.

Tips & Contingency

  • Agree the meeting point and a fallback in advance, and share live locations — signal is patchy among tall buildings.
  • One vaporetto day pass each covers the lagoon ferry and all Grand Canal hops — simplest for both families.
  • Beat the heat: hats, sun cream, refillable bottles (public fountains all over); keep the pace gentle and chase the shade.
  • Comfy shoes — endless stone and stepped bridges; a buggy is awkward here.
  • Watch the clock home: note the last good return ferry (Punta Sabbioni) and train (Santa Lucia) before you set off — they differ for each family.
  • It’s a domestic trip — no passport needed, but keep ID and booking details handy.
