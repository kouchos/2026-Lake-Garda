A flexible day-by-day framework for the 10 nights (Sat 27 June – Tue 7 July 2026), built from everything on the other pages. It is a suggestion, not a schedule — shuffle days around the weather and how tired everyone is.

The Golden Rules

  • Big parks & excursions mid-week (Tue–Fri). Weekends fill with local families — keep Sat/Sun for the pool, the lake and Peschiera. See the crowd strategy on the Activities page.
  • Do the must-dos in week one — it is slightly quieter before northern-European schools break up around 4 July.
  • Mornings are gold. Cooler for parks, calmer water for boats, shorter queues, and the best light for a run. Afternoon thunderstorms are common (usually 30–60 min) — have an indoor plan ready.
  • Build in pool days. Don’t stack two big days back-to-back with three children.

Wet-Weather / Rest-Day Swaps

If a storm rolls in or everyone needs a slower day, swap in one of these:

  • Movieland at CanevaWorld — indoor rides and stunt shows work on a cloudy day.
  • Verona — museums, the Arena and covered arcades; trains run all day.
  • Resort games room / indoor bar — the bolt-hole for a short, sharp downpour.
  • A long lazy lunch at one of the Dining picks while it passes.

Before You Lock Anything In

  • Buy theme-park tickets online in advance (cheaper, skips the gate queue).
  • Check the market day for each town on the Markets page and pin the right morning.
  • Note the last useful return train before any Venice/Verona day.
  • Glance at the Essentials page once so you know the emergency numbers and nearest pharmacy.

This is a planning framework only — confirm opening days, market days, ticket prices and train times close to each date.
