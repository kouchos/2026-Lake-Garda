The set-up: a family of 5 — 2 adults + Rachel (~9) and twins Jessica & Rebecca (~7). At these ages all three children can ride their own bikes, so the realistic kit is 2 adult city/trekking bikes + 3 children's bikes, helmets included. The flat, traffic-free Mincio cycle path right beside Peschiera makes this one of the easiest family activities of the whole trip.

⚠️ Prices below are indicative 2026 estimates gathered from local rental sites (June 2026) and need confirming at the time of booking — summer rates, online discounts and family deals all move around. Treat them as a budgeting guide, not a quote.

Several rental shops sit within a short ride of Del Garda Village & Camping 📍 Map (Via Marzan 92). Most will also deliver to the campsite for a small fee — worth it for a family of 5 so you don't have to ride 5 bikes back through town.

5.2 What to Hire for the Five of Us

  • 2 × adult city / trekking bikes — comfortable, upright, fine for the lakefront and the Mincio path.
  • 1 × children's bike (~24") for Rachel (~9).
  • 2 × children's bikes (~20") for the twins (~7).
  • Helmets — usually included; confirm 3 child-size helmets. Not legally required in Italy but strongly recommended for the kids.
  • Optional: a child seat or trailer if either twin tires on a longer day — Velolake and Bella Italia both offer these.
  • Optional: 1 adult e-bike if you fancy a longer adult ride; adds roughly €15–25/day over a city bike.

5.4 Single Days vs an Extended Hire

  • Single days (as needed): best if cycling is a "if the weather's good" call. Pick a flat, traffic-free morning ride, hand the bikes back the same evening. Use a shop with partial-day pricing (Rent&Ride) or quick campsite delivery. Budget ~€60/day for the five of us.
  • Extended hire (3 / 5 / 7 days): far cheaper per day and the bikes live at the campsite, so you can ride on a whim — to town for a gelato, to the pool, along the lake before breakfast. Best value if you expect to ride 3+ days: a 5-day hire (~€225) costs about the same as four separate single days (~€240).
  • Rule of thumb: riding 1–2 days → pay per day; riding 3+ days → take the multi-day or weekly rate.

5.5 Where to Actually Ride (Family-Friendly)

All distances are from Del Garda Village & Camping (a little south of Peschiera town).

  1. Mincio Cycle Path (Ciclovia Peschiera–Mantova) 📍 Map — the star ride. Flat, paved/gravel, almost entirely traffic-free, following the River Mincio south. ~12–15 min cycle to the start at Porta Brescia. Ride as far as the kids fancy and turn back — or push on to the storybook village of Borghetto sul Mincio 📍 Map (~13 km one way) for lunch by the water.
  2. Lakefront to Lazise 📍 Map — gentle ride north along the lake to a pretty walled town with gelato and a harbour. ~25–30 min cycle.
  3. Pacengo harbour loop 📍 Map — short, flat lake promenade pootle (~5 km round trip), great for a first shake-down ride with the twins. ~10 min to the promenade.
  4. Into Peschiera old town 📍 Map — ~10 min cycle for the fortress walls and canals. Quietest early or mid-week (weekends get busy with locals).

5.6 Practical Tips

  • Book ahead in summer — late June/early July is peak; children's bikes in the right sizes sell out. Reserve online before you fly.
  • Ride early. It's often 25°C+ by 9am — aim for morning rides and shade in the afternoon.
  • Confirm at booking: 3 child-size helmets, the exact wheel sizes (≈20" twins, ≈24" Rachel), a lock per bike, and whether delivery to Del Garda Village, Via Marzan 92 is included.
  • Take water and sun cream — the Mincio path has cafés but long shadeless stretches.
