13. Dining Etiquette & Tipping

Key things to know before the bill arrives:

  • "Coperto" (Cover Charge):
    • Almost every restaurant adds this mandatory charge (usually €2 - €3 per person, even kids).
    • It covers bread, table linen, and silverware. It is not a tip.
  • "Servizio" (Service Charge):
    • Check the menu! Some tourist spots specifically add a 10-12% Servizio charge.
    • If you see Servizio Incluso on the bill, the tip is already included.
  • Tipping Rule of Thumb:
    • Not Mandatory: Tipping is much less common than in the US/UK. Staff are paid a living wage.
    • The "Round Up": The most common local practice is to simply round up the bill. (e.g., Bill is €47 -> Leave €50).
    • Exceptional Service: Leaving €5 - €10 on the table for a family meal is seen as a very generous gesture.
