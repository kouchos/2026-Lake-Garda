Beyond Peschiera, the lake is ringed with gorgeous little towns. The nicest way to reach most of them is the Navigazione Laghi ferry from Peschiera harbour Map — no parking stress, and the boat ride is half the fun (see Boat Hire). Here are the ones worth your time, closest first.

Ferry times and fares change seasonally — check the Navigazione Laghi timetable for your dates. Children travel at a reduced fare.

Sirmione — the Showstopper

Sirmione Map sits on a long thin peninsula and is the most spectacular town on the lake. Cars can’t enter the old town, so the ferry from Peschiera is ideal.

  • Scaliger Castle Map — a fairytale moated castle you can climb (great for the kids; some steep steps).
  • Grotte di Catullo Map — sprawling Roman villa ruins at the tip, with olive groves and lake views.
  • Jamaica Beach Map — famous flat rock “beach” with shallow turquoise water near the ruins. Water shoes essential.
  • Aquaria Thermal Spa Map — lakeside thermal pools (check the minimum age for children before planning a visit).
  • Family note: lovely but it gets very busy in peak season — go early or late, and keep small ones close on the narrow lanes.

Lazise — Closest & Easiest

Lazise Map is the nearest of the walled towns — a pretty harbour, medieval walls, a little castle and easy lakefront strolling. Plenty of gelato and family-friendly restaurants, and it’s right beside CanevaWorld. A relaxed half-day or evening out.

Bardolino & Garda

  • Bardolino Map — the wine town (of the light red of the same name), with a long lakefront promenade perfect for an evening passeggiata and a spritz. An olive-oil and wine museum nearby for a rainy hour.
  • Garda Map — lively and characterful, with a buzzy waterfront, good gelato and a big Friday market.

Malcesine & Monte Baldo (a Big Day)

Malcesine Map is the prettiest village on the upper lake, with another Scaliger castle right on the water. Its star turn is the Monte Baldo cable car Map — rotating cabins that climb to ~1,760 m for cool mountain air and huge views (a relief on a hot day). It’s a long way from Peschiera (~1 h+ each way), so treat it as a full-day trip and go early to beat the cable-car queues. (Verify cable-car running times and prices before you go.)

Tips for Town Days

  • Take the ferry where you can — no ZTL/parking hassle, and the kids love being on the boat.
  • Go early or after 5pm — the old towns bake and crowd at midday.
  • Eat two streets back from the waterfront for better food and prices (see Etiquette).
  • Pair a town with its market day — see the Markets page.
