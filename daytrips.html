Two world-class cities are within easy reach of the resort, and the best part is Peschiera del Garda has its own railway station Map (~2.5 km from Del Garda Village) on the main Milan–Venice line. Leave the car behind and let the train do the work.

Buy tickets at the station machine or the Trenitalia app. Children 4–11 travel at a reduced fare (often free with an adult on regional trains); under 4 free. Validate regional paper tickets before boarding. Fares flagged for verification.

Verona — the Easy One

Getting there: frequent regional trains to Verona Porta Nuova Map (~15–25 min). From the station it’s a 20-min walk or a short bus/taxi to the Arena. Driving is ~30 min but the centre is a ZTL (restricted) — park at a garage like Arena/Cittadella and walk.

One perfect day (with kids):

  1. Arena di Verona Map — the Roman amphitheatre; climb the steps for big views (~€12 adult, kids reduced/free).
  2. Piazza Bra & Via Mazzini — cafés and the main shopping street.
  3. Casa di Giulietta Map — Juliet’s balcony (the courtyard is free; pay to go up).
  4. Piazza delle Erbe Map — lively market square; ride the lift up Torre dei Lamberti for the panorama.
  5. Castelvecchio & its bridge, then Ponte Pietra and a gelato by the river.

Short on time? Arena → Piazza delle Erbe → gelato → Castelvecchio covers the magic in half a day.

Where to eat (3 picks):

  • Pizzeria Du de Cope Map — bright, modern, brilliant pizza; the easy family choice.
  • Trattoria al Pompiere Map — classic Veronese cooking and famous cured meats (book ahead).
  • Osteria al Duca Map — hearty local dishes in the “House of Romeo,” good value.

Venice — the Big Adventure

Getting there: direct regional trains run to Venezia Santa Lucia Map in ~1h45–2h; for speed, take a regional to Verona then a Frecciarossa/Italo to Venice (~1h10 that leg, book ahead for the best fares). The station steps lead straight onto the Grand Canal — no cars in Venice at all.

One full day (with kids):

  1. Vaporetto Line 1 Map down the Grand Canal — the ride itself is the star attraction (buy a 24-hour pass, ~€25 pp; kids reduced).
  2. Rialto Bridge & market Map.
  3. Piazza San Marco Map — the Basilica and Campanile (book a Basilica slot online to skip the queue).
  4. Get lost on purpose in a quieter sestiere; chase a gelato; browse the mask shops.
  5. Optional gondola (~€90 for 30 min, up to 5 share the boat) — a splurge but unforgettable.

Got extra energy? A vaporetto to Burano (colourful fishermen’s houses) is a kid-pleaser, but it adds ~1h each way — only with an early start.

Where to eat (3 picks):

  • Rosticceria Gislon Map — classic cheap-and-quick Venetian counter food near Rialto; great with kids.
  • Trattoria alla Madonna Map — traditional Venetian seafood, a local institution (cash-friendly, busy).
  • Birraria La Corte Map — pizza/pasta on a big square with room to breathe — family-friendly.

Day-Trip Tips

  • Start early — especially for Venice. Note the time of the last useful return train before you set off.
  • Avoid restaurants right on the main squares — cover charges and tourist prices; walk two streets back.
  • Beat the heat: carry refillable water bottles (both cities have public fountains), hats and sun cream.
  • Comfy shoes — lots of walking on stone and (in Venice) bridges with steps; a buggy is awkward there.
  • It’s a domestic trip — no passport needed, but keep ID and your hotel/booking details handy.
