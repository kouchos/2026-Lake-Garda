Quick Pronunciation Tips

  • c before e/i sounds like “ch” (ciao = chow); before a/o/u it’s a hard “k”.
  • ch is a hard “k” (Chianti = kee-AN-tee).
  • gli sounds like “lly” in million; gn like “ny” in canyon.
  • Every vowel is pronounced — grazie has three syllables.
  • A friendly Buongiorno on entering a shop or bar is expected — it’s just good manners.

Tip: let each child learn one phrase (Rachel: ordering the gelato; the twins: grazie and ciao) — the staff will love it.
