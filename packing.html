A family-first checklist for 10 nights at Del Garda Village (late June – early July). It’s hot by day but evenings can turn cool after a storm, so pack for sun, water and the odd downpour. Tick as you go.

Documents & Money

  • ☐ Passports (valid well beyond the trip) × 5
  • ☐ GHIC/EHIC cards and travel insurance documents
  • ☐ Aer Lingus flight confirmations (out 27 Jun, back 7 Jul)
  • ☐ Accommodation booking ref 2026011245404197
  • ☐ Airport transfer (Ziptransfers) confirmation
  • ☐ Gardaland / CanevaWorld tickets (if pre-booked)
  • ☐ Driving licence (if hiring a car) + booking
  • ☐ Euros in cash + debit/credit cards (tell your bank you’re travelling)
  • ☐ Photos/photocopies of key docs on your phone

Clothing (per person)

  • ☐ T-shirts / light tops & shorts; a couple of light dresses
  • ☐ Swimwear × 2–3 each (always one drying)
  • ☐ Lightweight rain jacket / Mac-in-a-sac — essential for Garda’s afternoon storms
  • ☐ One warm layer (hoodie/cardigan) for cool post-storm evenings
  • ☐ Sun hats & sunglasses for everyone
  • ☐ Comfortable walking shoes/trainers + sandals/flip-flops
  • ☐ Water shoes — many lake beaches are pebbly, not sandy
  • ☐ One smart-casual outfit each for dinners out
  • ☐ Underwear, socks, pyjamas

Beach & Pool

  • ☐ Towels (check what the mobile home provides — often bring/rent your own)
  • ☐ Goggles; armbands/floats for the twins; a small inflatable or two
  • ☐ Beach bag + a dry bag for phones/keys
  • ☐ Foldable sun shade / UV pop-up for the kids

Sun & Health

  • ☐ High-SPF sun cream (50+ for the children) + after-sun/aloe
  • ☐ Insect repellent — lakeside mosquitoes bite in the evening
  • ☐ Children’s + adult paracetamol/ibuprofen, antihistamine, plasters, antiseptic
  • ☐ Rehydration sachets, motion-sickness tablets (winding roads & boats)
  • ☐ Any prescription medicines (in hand luggage) + a thermometer
  • ☐ Small first-aid kit, hand sanitiser, tweezers

Toiletries

  • ☐ Travel-size shampoo, shower gel, toothbrushes & paste, deodorant, brush/comb, razor
  • ☐ Lip balm with SPF; any contact-lens supplies
  • ☐ Remember: liquids <100 ml in cabin bags

Kids’ Extras

  • ☐ Tablet + headphones with downloaded shows/films for the flight & transfer
  • ☐ Comfort toy each; colouring/activity books; a few favourite snacks
  • ☐ One spare change of clothes each in the hand luggage

Electronics

  • ☐ Phones + chargers; a power bank
  • ☐ EU travel adapter (Italy uses Type F/L plugs)
  • ☐ Camera; headphones; e-reader

Day Bag for Excursions

  • ☐ Backpack + refillable water bottles (fountains in Verona/Venice/Peschiera)
  • ☐ Sun hats, sun cream, wet wipes, hand sanitiser, mini first-aid
  • ☐ Snacks and a little cash for gelato

Self-Catering (Mobile Home)

  • ☐ Reusable shopping bags; a few first-night groceries (use the resort shop)
  • ☐ Tea/coffee favourites; ziplock bags; a little travel laundry detergent
  • ☐ Check what kitchenware/bedding the Clivia Medium includes before duplicating

Running Kit (if you’re running)

  • ☐ Trainers (with grip for the gravel river path), 3–4 light running sets, cap, handheld bottle
  • ☐ See the Running Guide for routes and timing

Hand-Luggage “Don’t Pack in the Hold”

  • ☐ Passports, money, travel docs, medicines
  • ☐ One change of clothes each, swim stuff (in case the hold bag is delayed)
  • ☐ Phone chargers, sunglasses, kids’ entertainment
  • ☐ Check your Aer Lingus cabin/hold baggage allowance before you pack

Tip: lay everything out, then halve the “maybe” pile — there’s a supermarket near the resort for anything you forget.
