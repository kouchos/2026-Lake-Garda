## 4. Activities Guide

### Crowd Prediction: "The Sweet Spot"
You have timed this very well.
*   **Italy:** Schools closed (they finish mid-June). Expect lots of domestic tourists, especially on weekends.
*   **Germany (Bavaria):** Schools are **OPEN**. Bavarian holidays don't start until August. This is huge, as Germans make up a massive chunk of Lake Garda tourism.
*   **Netherlands:** Schools in the North Break up July 4th. You might see a slight increase in Dutch plates in the car park towards the very end of your trip.
*   **Verdict:** Busy, but not "August Crazy".

**Strategy:**
*   **Weekends (Sat/Sun):** The lake roads and Gardaland will be packed with locals. Use these days for the pool or walking into Peschiera.
*   **Weekdays:** Best for Gardaland and driving to Sirmione/Lazise.
*   **Early vs Late:** The first week (June 27 - July 3) should be slightly quieter than your final few days (July 4-7) as Northern European schools start to break up. Do your "must-do" big excursions early in the week.

### 🏃 Running & Exercise Guide (The "Realistic" List)
*To beat the heat (often 25°C+ by 9am), plan your runs for **06:30 - 07:30**.*

1.  **Route A: The Pacengo Harbor Loop (~5km)**
    *   **The Route:** Turn **LEFT** out of the campsite gate to hit the lake promenade. Run North until you hit the little harbor at **Pacengo**, then turn back.
    *   **Terrain:** Paved, flat, hugging the water.
    *   **Tip:** Go *early* (before 8am). After that, it fills with pedestrians.

2.  **Route B: The Mincio 5k (Out & Back)**
    *   **The Route:** Head to Porta Brescia and hop on the *Ciclovia Mantova*. Run South for ~2.5km (until you see the first weir/dam), then loop back.
    *   **Terrain:** Gravel/Paved path, tree-lined river bank. Safe from cars.

3.  **Route C: The Fortress Walls Loop (~4.5km)**
    *   **The Route:** Run from campsite to town -> Do one full loop on top of the historic red brick walls -> Run home.
    *   **Terrain:** Mixed (Paved/Cobbles). Great views!

### Weather & Packing (Late June / Early July)
*   **The Vibe:** Hot summer days, but unpredictable evenings.
*   **Temperatures:**
    *   Day: **26°C - 30°C** (Perfect pool weather).
    *   Night: **18°C - 20°C** (Comfortable for sleeping).
*   **The "Lake Garda Storms":**
    *   Thunderstorms are *very common* in late afternoon/evening. They are intense, loud, but usually pass quickly (30-60 mins).
    *   **Packing Essential:** A lightweight **Rain Jacket / Mac-in-a-sac** is mandatory. Do not assume it will be dry for 10 days straight.
*   **Packing List Extras:**
    *   **Water Shoes:** Many lake beaches are pebbly (not sandy).
    *   **Mosquito Repellent:** Essential for evenings by the lake.
    *   **Cardigan/Hoodie:** It can actually feel cool after a storm in the evening.

## 7. Must-Try Food & Drink Guide
*A checklist of local specialties to try during your stay.*

### 🍽️ Foods
1.  **Carbonara (Roman Style):**
    *   *What:* Pasta with egg, pecorino cheese, guanciale (pork cheek), and pepper. No cream!
    *   *Best Place:* **Il Cantinone** (for authentic Roman) or **La Plume** (for a great version with a view).
2.  **Tortellini di Valeggio ("Love Knots"):**
    *   *What:* Paper-thin handmade pasta filled with meat.
    *   *Why:* A local legend from the nearby village of Valeggio. Usually served with butter and sage.
2.  **Bigoli with Duck Ragu (or Sardines):**
    *   *What:* Thick, rough spaghetti-like pasta beloved in Veneto.
    *   *Why:* The texture holds sauce perfectly. Duck ragu is the meaty favorite; "Sarde" (Lake Sardines) is the traditional fisherman's dish.
3.  **Gelato (Every Day!):**
    *   *What:* Italian Ice Cream.
    *   *Why:* It's lower fat and air than normal ice cream, meaning more intense flavour. Look for "Artigianale" (Handmade) signs.
4.  **Fritole (If lucky):**
    *   *What:* Small Venetian fried doughnuts, often found in bakeries.

### 🍕 Pizza to Look For
*Standard "Margherita" is always available, but try these local favourites:*
1.  **Pizza Monte Baldo (or "Ai Funghi"):**
    *   *Toppings:* Wild mushrooms (porcini) and sometimes truffle oil/shavings from the local mountain.
2.  **Pizza Trentina (or "Tirolese"):**
    *   *Toppings:* Speck (smoked ham), Brie, and sometimes Porcini. A nod to the Austrian influence nearby.
3.  **Pizza Radicchio e Gorgonzola:**
    *   *Toppings:* Red chicory (bitter/sweet) with blue cheese. A classic Veneto flavour profile.
4.  **Pizza con la Luganega:**
    *   *Toppings:* Tomato, Mozzarella and "Luganega" (Local sausage meat).

### 🍰 Dolci (Desserts)
1.  **Torta Sbrisolona:**
    *   *What:* A crumbly, almond biscuit-cake (originally from Mantua).
    *   *Why:* It’s not sliced, but "broken" into chunks. Great for sharing.
2.  **Torta delle Rose ("Rose Cake"):**
    *   *What:* A bouquet of sweet leavened dough rolls, rich in butter and sugar.
    *   *Why:* Created for a royal wedding in the region in 1490. Beautiful and soft.
3.  **Cassata Gardesana:**
    *   *What:* Not to be confused with the Sicilian version. This uses local ricotta, honey, and candied fruit.
    *   *Why:* Often served with a drizzle of local olive oil (yes, really!).
4.  **Biscotti al Limone:**
    *   *What:* Soft lemon cookies made with lemons from the lake shores (Limone sul Garda).

3.  **Aperol Spritz:**
    *   *What:* Prosecco, Aperol, Soda Water & Orange slice.
    *   *Why:* The orange sunset drink. It is *the* drink of 6pm in Italy.
4.  **Amarone della Valpolicella:**
    *   *What:* A powerful, rich red wine made from dried grapes.
    *   *Why:* The "King" of Verona wines. Sip it slowly after dinner.

### 🥤 Drinks (Non-Alcoholic)
1.  **Crodino:**
    *   *What:* The famous non-alcoholic aperitif (orange colour).
    *   *Why:* Let the kids (or non-drinkers) join "Aperitivo hour". It’s herbal, sparkling, and sweet-bitter.
2.  **Chinotto:**
    *   *What:* A dark soda made from the Chinotto citrus fruit.
    *   *Why:* Italy’s answer to Cola, but more complex and less sugary-sweet. A cult classic.
3.  **Granita:**
    *   *What:* Semi-frozen dessert drink made from sugar, water and various flavorings.
    *   *Why:* Lemon or Mint granita is the ultimate cool-down on a hot day.

## 8. Dining Etiquette & Tipping
*Key things to know before the bill arrives:*

*   **"Coperto" (Cover Charge):**
    *   Almost every restaurant adds this mandatory charge (usually **€2 - €3 per person**, even kids).
    *   It covers bread, table linen, and silverware. It is **not** a tip.
*   **"Servizio" (Service Charge):**
    *   Check the menu! Some tourist spots specifically add a 10-12% *Servizio* charge.
    *   If you see *Servizio Incluso* on the bill, the tip is already included.
*   **Tipping Rule of Thumb:**
    *   **Not Mandatory:** Tipping is much less common than in the US/UK. Staff are paid a living wage.
    *   **The "Round Up":** The most common local practice is to simply round up the bill. (e.g., Bill is €47 -> Leave €50).
    *   **Exceptional Service:** Leaving €5 - €10 on the table for a family meal is seen as a very generous gesture.
